# Research

My research and technical work focuses on air quality modeling, emissions analysis, monitoring, and machine learning.
The common theme is converting complex emissions, meteorology, monitoring, and activity datasets into defensible
scientific and regulatory evidence.

## Scientific questions

- How do mobile-source technology transitions change emissions inventories and regulatory planning assumptions?
- How can monitoring QA/QC, modeling, and machine learning be integrated without losing interpretability?
- Which VOC and PM<sub>2.5</sub> sources dominate exposure and ozone formation potential in oil and gas influenced regions?
- How can source apportionment methods be scaled to larger datasets while maintaining stability and physical meaning?

## Current and recent themes

### Mobile-source emissions and EMFAC

At CARB, my current technical work includes light-duty vehicle activity, fleet characterization, and emission factor
parameterization for EMFAC-related regulatory analysis. This work supports emissions inventory development and State
Implementation Plan applications.

### Oil and gas impacts on VOCs and ozone formation

My doctoral and publication work examined ambient hydrocarbons, VOC source contributions, and ozone formation potential
in North Texas, including the Barnett Shale region.

### PM<sub>2.5</sub> composition and source distribution

Recent research includes PM<sub>2.5</sub> composition and source distribution changes in an industrialized coastal urban
airshed in South Texas.

### Monitoring and QA/QC

I have operated and analyzed datasets from NOx and ozone analyzers, AQMesh micro-sensors, SASS, high-volume samplers,
and PEMS systems. This field experience informs my approach to model validation and emissions inventory evaluation.
