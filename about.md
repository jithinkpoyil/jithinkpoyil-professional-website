# About

I am an atmospheric scientist and air quality specialist working on emissions inventories, atmospheric modeling,
ambient monitoring, and data-driven air pollution analysis. My current work at the California Air Resources Board
focuses on mobile-source emissions analysis, EMFAC model development, light-duty fleet characterization, and emission
factor updates for regulatory inventory and planning applications.

My academic training is in mechanical and energy engineering, environmental engineering, and civil engineering. Across
public agency, university, transportation research, and consulting roles, I have worked on regulatory emissions
inventories, mobile-source testing, dispersion modeling, receptor modeling, machine-learning forecasting, air toxics
analysis, ambient monitoring QA/QC, and technical reporting.

## Professional identity

My work is centered on three connected questions:

1. How can emissions and activity data be converted into defensible regulatory inventories?
2. How do emissions from transportation, oil and gas, industry, and combustion sources affect ambient air quality?
3. How can physical modeling, monitoring, and machine learning be integrated without losing interpretability or regulatory usefulness?

## Research orientation

My research combines source-resolved measurements, emissions modeling, regulatory datasets, and statistical learning. I have worked on PM<sub>2.5</sub>, VOCs, ozone formation potential, oil and gas impacts, regional emissions inventories, and deep-learning methods for air quality forecasting and source apportionment.
